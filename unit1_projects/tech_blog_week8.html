    • Regular Expressions

    During week 4 we were introduced to regular expression, I applied my limited knowledge to to solve part of the cipher challenge. Over the next few weeks as I reviewed my cohorts members challenges I would come across them on and off. I knew how effective and simple they looked but was too intimidated to get into it fully. So I decided to learn more about regular expressions. Having gone through many resources, I decided the focus on the basic mechanics of how they work. In this blog I will mostly cover simple extractions, booleans and switching. As a motion designer I have used simple expression to do some repetitive task with different parameters to do repetitive tasks that would have taken me many many hours to keyframe and animate. Some of them were pretty simple single line codes, but they made the motion design look really spectacular. There are four basic types of expressions, the boolean expressions, which evaluate to true or false; integer expression, which evaluates whole numbers; float expressions, which evaluate real numbers, and finally string expressions, which evaluates characters of a string.

    In Ruby, regular expression is a powerful tool that can be used with strings and text. With regular expressions you can test, extract or change the given string. Regexp is a way of creating a pattern of characters that can be matched to a string. You write a regular expression between slash characters. Like everything in Ruby, Regexp is an object. since it is an object, you can assign it to a variable, pass it to methods and also send messages. I am going to use some simple examples to show how you can do some specific searches, assign the search result to a variable and apply methods to it.
