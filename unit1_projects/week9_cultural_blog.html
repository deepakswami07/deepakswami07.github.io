    • Learning Style Revisited

    My thinking style is concrete sequential. On further researching I found out that most teachers fall into this category. Having been a teacher for several years I function very well in a structured environment. I am very practical and also detail oriented. I like to solve problems in a sequential step-by-step progression. Since a lot of coding problems are solved sequentially and paying attention to detail is also important, I can use these skills to my advantage at DBC.

    When I think of everything that I have learned so far at DBC, the two topics that I had difficulty with were JS and SQL. Now when I look back on why I struggled with these two topics, I see that I was better able to understand Ruby because I had ample time to go over it before coming to DBC. With JS and SQL I did not spend enough time clearly understanding the basics, especially with SQL which I felt was stuck in with other topics. This is partly because of the way the lessons were structured but also because of my learning style. I alway like to learn the basics before a topic is introduced and need time to reflect, but as we were moving from one topic to another it became difficult to reflect and compare.

    Even though I have particular type of learning style,I believe there are more ways to learn and my learning style can morph and change. One change that I have already made and see the benefit is solving problems by pairing. Initially I was very nervous and intimidated by the thought of pairing. I am a very independent learner, so this change has been an eye opener for me. I am going to use failure as a springboard to explore why I failed and think of other ways of solving the problem or issues I might face when I am at DBC. My overall approach so far has been irrespective of the the struggles I have with a topic with every passing day I have a better understanding of the topic.
